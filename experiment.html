The experiment

This experiment intends to answer, in a manner accessible to average door users, whether their choice of a revolving door versus regular might justifiably be thought to reduce energy consumption.

Basically, this uses a small-scale model of a building equipped with both a revolving door and a swinging door. The heated tiny building sits inside a climactic chamber, that is, a freezer, and automatically opens its doors in proportion to a rate of traffic.

By measuring the drop in temperature caused by different numbers of simulated people arriving, we should see whether, all else equal, the revolving door expends less heat.

Model construction and programming is still in progress, more details to follow
